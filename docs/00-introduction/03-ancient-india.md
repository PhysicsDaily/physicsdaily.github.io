# Ancient India: Pioneers of Atomism and Astronomy

Beyond the Vedas, the classical period of Indian science produced giants whose contributions are often overlooked in standard Western textbooks.

### Kanada and the Atomic Theory
Sage **Kanada** (c. 6th century BCE), founder of the **Vaisheshika** school of philosophy, formulated a theory of atoms (*Parmanu*) long before Democritus.
*   He postulated that matter is not continuous but made of indivisible particles.
*   He categorized these atoms by their properties (earth, water, fire, air) and suggested they combine in specific ways (diatomic and triatomic structures) to form the macroscopic world.
*   **Quote:** *"Every object of creation is made of atoms which in turn connect with each other to form molecules."*

### Aryabhata & The Golden Age
*   **Aryabhata (476–550 CE):** In his magnum opus *Aryabhatiya*, he explicitly stated that the **Earth rotates on its axis**, causing day and night—a revolutionary idea when most of the world believed the heavens rotated around a static Earth. He also calculated the circumference of the Earth with astonishing accuracy.
*   **Zero (Shunya):** The concept of zero as a number (not just a placeholder) and the decimal system were gifts from India to the world. Without this mathematical framework, modern physics computation would be impossible.
