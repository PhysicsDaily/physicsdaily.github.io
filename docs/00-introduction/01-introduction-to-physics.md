# Introduction to Physics

**Physics** is the fundamental natural science that explores the deepest questions about the universe. It is the study of **matter, energy, space, and time**, and how they interact. From the smallest subatomic particles to the largest galaxies, physics seeks to uncover the universal laws that govern everything around us. It's an experimental science, relying on observation, measurement, and mathematical modeling to describe and predict phenomena.

### Why Study Physics?

Studying physics is not just about learning equations; it is about satisfying our fundamental curiosity to know *why* the universe behaves the way it does. Beyond this intellectual fulfillment, physics forms the bedrock of all modern technology—from the smartphone in your pocket to medical imaging devices. It sharpens critical thinking, teaching us to break down complex problems into solvable parts, and provides the essential foundation for understanding all other natural sciences, including chemistry, biology, and engineering.
